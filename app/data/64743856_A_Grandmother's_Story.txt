A Grandmother's Story (), also known as Au pays des Jouets, is a 1908 French short silent film by Georges Méliès. It was sold by Méliès's Star Film Company and is numbered 1314–1325 in its catalogues. The child in the film is played by Méliès's son, André Méliès, who was seven years old at the time. The film's special effects include stage machinery, substitution splices, multiple exposures, and dissolves.
